## Legal vs. Illegal IPTV Use

### ✅ Legal Uses (Examples)

- **Paid IPTV Services**: Legitimate providers like YouTube TV, Sling TV, Hulu Live, etc.
- **Self-Hosted Content**: Your own cameras, media servers, or personal video files
- **Free-to-Air Channels**: Publicly broadcast channels with authorized streaming
- **Official Broadcaster Streams**: Channels that provide legal M3U exports
- **Licensed Content**: Any content you have explicit permission to stream

### ❌ Illegal Uses (Examples)

- **Pirated Premium Channels**: HBO, ESPN, Sky Sports, etc. without authorization
- **"Free IPTV" Lists**: Many contain stolen streams from paid services
- **Unauthorized Rebroadcasts**: Restreaming copyrighted content without license
- **Pay-Per-View Events**: Accessing PPV content without paying

**If you're unsure whether a playlist is legal, assume it is not.**

## Regional Legal Considerations

### United States
Streaming copyrighted content without authorization violates:
- Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA)
- Copyright Act of 1976
- May result in civil lawsuits or criminal prosecution

### European Union
Accessing copyrighted content without authorization violates:
- EU Copyright Directive (2019/790)
- Individual member state copyright laws
- ECJ ruling in *Filmspeler* case (2017) confirms illegality

### United Kingdom
Unauthorized streaming violates:
- Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988
- Digital Economy Act 2017
- Can result in fines up to £50,000 or imprisonment

### Canada
Unauthorized streaming violates:
- Copyright Act (R.S.C., 1985, c. C-42)
- May result in civil penalties

### Australia
Unauthorized streaming violates:
- Copyright Act 1968
- Broadcasting Services Act 1992
- Can result in fines up to AUD $117,000

**This list is not exhaustive. Check your local laws.**
